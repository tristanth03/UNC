1. Level Design

1.1 Notation

\(\mathcal{X}_{i,j}\) refers to the domain in which the operator \(T_{i,j}\) acts on and \(\mathcal{Y}_{i,j}\) is the co-domain, for instance if for some \(i,j\), \(\mathcal{X}_{i,j}=\{1,2\}\) then the operator

\[T_{i,j}: \mathcal{X}_{i,j}\times \mathcal{X}_{i,j} \to \mathcal{Y}_{i,j}\]

suppose that specific operator is a plus then \(\mathcal{Y}_{i,j} = \{1,2,3,4\}\)

1.3 Levels

Level 1-2

The plus operator is introduced in a simplistic setting, with \(\mathcal{X}_{1,1} = \{1,...,5\}\) hence \(\mathcal{Y}_{1,1} = \{2,...,10\}\)

Level 3-5

The domain's cardinality is increased (hence the co-domain's): \(\mathcal{X}_{1,2} = \{0,...,10\}\) and \(\mathcal{Y}_{1,2} = \{0,\dots,20\} \)
